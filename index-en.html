Authoritarian Regimes

Within the perception of political science, authoritarianism has as its main characteristics the concentration and exclusivity of the exercise of power by a single person, or a group, to the detriment of representative institutions. Thus, political systems that do not guarantee democracy in the social environment, characterizing an undemocratic governmental political structure.

In George Orwell's 1984 novel, set in London, in fictional Party-ruled Oceania Ingsoc. The supreme entity and symbol of strength and invincibility, Big Brother, is stamped everywhere. Society is imposed under a totalitarian regime, in which through government mechanisms such as: the thought police, telescreens, anti-sex league, Novolingua and others, the company remains controlled according to the wishes of the Party.
