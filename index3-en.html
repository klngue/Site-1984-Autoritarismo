Authoritarian regimes in the present

After years of the end of World War II, a time when the world was able to observe governments with authoritarian characteristics, such as the Nazi regime in Germany, it is common sense to believe that there are no authoritarian regimes in force in the countries.

However, a survey carried out by the Intelligence Unit of the newspaper The Economist that analyzes electoral processes and pluralism, civil liberties, government functioning, political participation and political culture, to divide countries into 4 classifications, namely: Full Democracy, Defective Democracies, Regimes Hybrids and Authoritarian Regimes.
